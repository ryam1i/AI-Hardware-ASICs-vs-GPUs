AI Chips (ASICs) vs. GPUs

Comparative Analysis in High-Performance Computing

Bachelor Year 1 | Final Exam

What are we comparing?

GPU (Graphics Processing Unit)

The "Generalist". Designed for graphics but adapted for parallel math. High flexibility, medium efficiency.

ASIC (Application-Specific IC)

The "Specialist". Custom hardware for one task (e.g. Google TPU). Zero flexibility, maximum efficiency.

The Architecture Gap

1 GPU Architecture

Relies on thousands of SIMD (Single Instruction, Multiple Data) cores. Great at parallel math but heavy on "Control Logic" and memory fetch cycles.

2 ASIC (Systolic Array)

Data flows through cells like blood in a heart. Reduces memory access by passing results directly to the next neighbor. Pure math, no fluff.

The "Hardware Lottery"

Research by Sara Hooker (2021) suggests that AI breakthroughs are often decided by what hardware is available, not just what idea is better.

GPU Path

"I have a new idea for a neural network. I can test it immediately because my GPU is programmable."

Winner: Innovation

ASIC Path

"I have a specific model I need to run for 10 billion users. I will build a chip just for this."

Winner: Scale
